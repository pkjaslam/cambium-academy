The course · four habits

Agents that do the work, and cannot do damage

Course 05's thesis, defended by all twelve modules: making an agent look impressive takes an afternoon; making one you can trust on real inputs is the actual job, and it is the job almost everyone skips. Most pilots die not for want of a better model, but for want of scoping, testing, and supervision. Four habits fix that.

  • Scope well: automate the repetitive, verifiable, bounded, low-blast-radius jobs. Keep the irreversible, high-stakes, and unverifiable in human hands.
  • Wire well: an agent can only do what its tools allow, so the tool menu is your first safety control. MCP is the emerging standard; Claude, ChatGPT, Zapier, and n8n get you started free.
  • Test well: build a golden set of real cases with known-good answers, and rerun it on every change. Green today is not green tomorrow. This is the step the 88% skipped.
  • Supervise well: least privilege, a small blast radius, and a human gate before anything irreversible. You cannot prompt your way out of prompt injection; the defense is architecture.
  • Own the outcome: an agent can do the work, but a human always owns the result and the upkeep. Ship in stages, keep a kill switch, and stand behind what it does.
