Do it for real

Make your first AI agent in Claude

An "agent" sounds complicated. It is not. It is just an AI you have given a job, some knowledge, and a few tools, saved so you can reuse it. Here is how to build a real one in Claude in about five minutes. No code.

What you need: a Claude account (the free plan works to try it; saving a Project needs Claude Pro). Open claude.ai in another tab and follow along.
A 90-second walkthrough of all five steps. The written steps below are the full guide, follow them in your own Claude tab.
1

Create a Project

In Claude, open Projects in the left sidebar and click New project. Give it a name and a one-line goal. A Project is just a saved workspace: your agent lives here, and it remembers its job every time.

2

Give it a job (the instructions)

Open the Project and set its Custom instructions. This is the whole trick. Write a short job description: who it is, what it does, its rules. Claude follows this in every chat inside the Project, so you never re-explain.

Grant Helper · Instructions
Custom instructions
You are a grant-writing assistant for an ecology lab. Draft clear, honest sections from my notes. Never invent citations or results. Ask before assuming a number. Keep the tone plain and specific.
Save instructions
Think of it as a job description, not a magic spell. Plain and specific beats clever.
3

Give it knowledge (your files)

Upload the documents your agent should always know: a style guide, past reports, a data file. Drop them in the Project's Files panel. Now every answer is grounded in your material, not the whole internet.

4

Give it tools (connectors)

Want it to reach your real apps, your calendar, your drive, the web? Add a connector. Claude's directory has hundreds, and each is a few clicks, no setup. This is what turns a chatbot into an agent that can actually do things.

Course rule: only add the tools this job truly needs, and turn on approval for anything it cannot undo (like sending or deleting). That one habit is the whole safety lesson.
5

Run it

Start a chat inside the Project and give it a real task. It already knows its job, reads your files, and can use its tools. That is your agent, running. Reuse it any time by opening the Project again.

Grant Helper · Chat
Draft the Significance section from my field notes, in our house style.
Done. I used lab-style-guide.pdf and pulled the 2025 numbers from your CSV. I left two figures blank and flagged them, since your notes did not confirm them. Want me to tighten the opening?

The one rule to carry with you

An agent is only as safe as the tools you gave it. Give it the fewest tools for the job, and make anything irreversible ask you first. You do not need the agent to be perfect; you need its worst mistake to be impossible or easy to catch. That is the whole of this course, in one sentence.

Same idea, any tool. This exact pattern, a job, some knowledge, a few scoped tools, is how you build an agent in ChatGPT (a Custom GPT), Google Gemini (a Gem), or a no-code tool like Zapier or Copilot Studio. Learn it once in Claude and you can build one anywhere.
