THE REFERENCE AUDITOR

The Lab taught you the 1-in-277 problem on a rigged reference list. This is the real instrument: paste your own bibliography and it resolves every DOI against the live Crossref registry, the same registry an editor's screening tools use. Phantom references surface in seconds. Run it on your next manuscript before a reviewer does.

What a PHANTOM verdict means, and what it does not. It means Crossref has no record of that DOI: the identifier resolves to nothing. That is exactly how an AI-invented citation behaves, and it is how an editor's tooling will see it. It does not automatically mean somebody fabricated it: a typo, a mangled copy-paste, or a very new preprint can all produce the same result. So do what this whole course teaches: treat the flag as a question, open the reference yourself, and settle it with evidence.
Privacy: your text is parsed in your browser. The only thing that leaves this page is the bare DOI string of each reference, sent to api.crossref.org to ask whether it exists. Your manuscript, your notes, and your unpublished titles are never transmitted. If even that is too much, the Local Referee runs with no network at all.
PART 2 · CLICK THREE, AUTOMATED

Does the paper you cited actually say it?

The subtlest failure is not the fake citation; it is the real one that does not support the sentence you hung on it. Paste your claim and the abstract of the paper you cited. A language model runs inside your browser (nothing is sent anywhere) and measures how closely the two are related.

First run downloads a small model (about 23 MB), then works offline.
Honest about what this measures. It measures semantic relatedness, not logical support. A low score is a strong signal that you should reopen the paper; a high score is not a certificate that the citation is sound, because a paper can be closely related and still say the opposite. This is a smoke alarm, not a judge. The judgment stays yours, which is the seventh of the irreducible seven.
