# On-page board openers (seed the community page)

The three boxes on the community page (Q&A board, Discussion space, Graduate wall) are giscus widgets. Each is its own thread and starts empty until someone comments. To make them feel alive from day one, sign in on the community page and paste each opener below as the **first comment** in its box.

**Community page:** https://pkjaslam.github.io/cambium-academy/courses/course-01-intro-to-ai/web/community.html
(First time only: click "Sign in with GitHub" in a box and authorize the giscus comments app. Then paste and Comment.)

## Q&A board  →  first comment
```
A point worth noting before you ask anything here: there is no basic question. The person who asks "what actually is a token?" usually understands it an hour later far better than the person who nodded and scrolled past.

So ask. A quiz question that stung, a line from the lecture that did not land, a model everyone keeps naming that you cannot quite place. Keep it short and specific and you will get the best answer, from Aira and from people who sat exactly where you are now.

And carry one habit out of this course: whenever an AI answers you, ask yourself how you would check it. That one question is most of what AI literacy means.
```

## Discussion space  →  first comment
```
A thought to open the room with: the most valuable AI skill in 2026 is not clever prompting. It is knowing when not to trust the answer. A model can sound completely fluent and be completely wrong in the same sentence, so the people who get the most from these tools are simply the ones who check before they rely.

Your turn. Tell us your name, where you are joining from, and one thing you want AI to help you do this month. Then tell us what you want taught next week. This Academy is built around that answer, so it is a real question, not a polite one.
```
