◈ PRACTICE WITH KNOWN ANSWERS

THE SCREENING ROOM

Three real desk tasks, each with a known right answer and a written explanation. Nothing here is graded and nothing gates your quiz. This is where you warm up before The Practical, and where you come back when you want to keep your judgment sharp.

EXERCISE 1 · REPAIR THE DISCLOSURE

This statement will not survive its journal

An author submits to an Elsevier-family journal. They used AI to polish their English, to restructure the discussion, and to draft the plotting code they later verified. Here is what they wrote. Find what is wrong with it.

Acknowledgments
"The authors would like to thank ChatGPT for its assistance with this manuscript, and acknowledge it as a contributor to the writing. AI was used throughout. We also used Microsoft Word's spellchecker, Zotero, Grammarly, Excel, and a calculator."
EXERCISE 2 · GRADE THE REVIEW

Two reviews of the same paper

You are the handling editor. Both referees returned on time. Which review can you actually act on, and why? Read both before you choose.

Referee 1
"This is a well-written and comprehensive manuscript that addresses an important topic in the field. The methodology is sound and the results are clearly presented. The authors have done a commendable job situating their work in the literature. The writing is polished and professional throughout. However, the manuscript reads as though it may have been written with AI assistance, and the prose has a certain uniformity that raises concerns. I recommend rejection on these grounds."
Referee 2
"The central claim rests on reference 14, which I could not locate: the DOI does not resolve and no database returns the title. Please provide it. Second, the sample described in the Methods (n = 40) does not match Table 1 (n = 52); the discrepancy is not explained. Third, the reported t(38) = 1.02 cannot yield p < .001; either the statistic or the p-value is in error, and this affects the primary conclusion. I recommend major revision, and I would be glad to see it again."
