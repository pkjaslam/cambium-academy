# Course 03 · Free learning resources

Every link below was verified live on July 12, 2026 by Cambium's librarian pass. If one breaks, open an issue on the repo. Ordered by learner journey, so start at the top.

## The verification habit (the heart of this course)

| Resource | Link | Format | Why it's worth it |
|---|---|---|---|
| SIFT: The Four Moves · Mike Caulfield | https://hapgood.us/2019/06/19/sift-the-four-moves/ | Blog post, no login | The verification habit this course teaches, straight from its inventor: Stop, Investigate the source, Find better coverage, Trace to the original. |
| Web Literacy for Student Fact-Checkers · Mike Caulfield | https://webliteracy.pressbooks.com/ | Free open textbook (CC-BY) | The full hands-on course in lateral reading. Short chapters, real examples, written for people who are not librarians. |
| News Literacy Project | https://newslit.org/ | Site, free | Practical checklists, lessons, and tools for verifying claims, sources, and images. |

## Checking sources and citations

| Resource | Link | Format | Why it's worth it |
|---|---|---|---|
| Google Scholar | https://scholar.google.com/ | Search, no login | The fastest way to confirm a paper exists, find the real venue and year, and see who cited it. Click 1 of the 3-click check. |
| Crossref guest query | https://www.crossref.org/guestquery/ | Lookup, no login | Paste a DOI or title and see the real registered record behind it. The registry the DOIs themselves live in. |
| Retraction Watch | https://retractionwatch.com/ | Site, free | Where the fabricated-citation story is being reported, paper by paper, including AI-generated fakes that made it into journals. |

## The facts behind the course's eye-openers

| Claim in the course | Where it comes from |
|---|---|
| Fabricated references rose from ~1 in 2,828 papers (2023) to ~1 in 277 (early 2026) | Lancet 2026 analysis of 2M+ papers / 97M citations, reported by STAT News, May 7, 2026 |
| AI search tools wrong on 60%+ of news-citation queries, while sounding confident | Columbia Journalism Review (Tow Center) test of eight AI search tools |
| Frontier models fabricate 18-28% of citations in literature-review tasks | 2026 reference-hallucination benchmarks (incl. arXiv 2604.03173 on deep-research agents) |

Facts and tool names are a July 2026 snapshot. The habits (receipts, lateral reading, opened citations) do not expire.
