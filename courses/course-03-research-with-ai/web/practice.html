STEP 2 · THE SIX TRAPS

What is hiding in this file

Answer as you find them. Every check below has one right answer, computed from the cleaned data. An AI assistant that skips any of these will hand you a plausible, confident, wrong result.

Trap 1 · Unit mix. Three readings at one site are recorded in Fahrenheit, not Celsius. Which site?
Trap 2 · Sentinel missing values. Missing readings are not blank; they are coded with a number that would poison every mean. What number?
Trap 3 · Duplicates. How many rows are exact duplicates that must be dropped?
Trap 4 · Contradictory metadata. One site's buffer width is mislabelled on two dates, contradicting the rest of its own rows. Which site?
Trap 5 · The clean row count. After fixing units, dropping sentinels and duplicates, how many rows remain?
Trap 6 · The answer that matters. Mean temperature at 15 m buffers minus mean at 30 m buffers, in degrees C, to one decimal place.

STEP 3 · THE SYSTEMATIC REVIEW, IN MINIATURE

A PRISMA-shaped workflow you can actually finish

The most common first serious task for researchers using AI. Full PRISMA is a project; this is the honest miniature, and it is the shape reviewers expect.

  1. Question, written as criteria. Population, exposure/intervention, comparator, outcome, study designs you will accept, years, languages. Write them down BEFORE searching, so the criteria cannot drift to fit what you find.
  2. Search, recorded. Run the same query in an academic agent (Elicit-class) and one database (Scopus, Web of Science, PubMed). Save the exact query strings and dates. A search you cannot reproduce is not a systematic search.
  3. Screen in two passes. Title/abstract, then full text. AI can pre-screen with your criteria, but every exclusion at full-text stage gets a human-recorded reason.
  4. Count everything. Records identified, duplicates removed, screened, excluded (with reasons), included. This is the PRISMA flow diagram, and it is non-negotiable.
  5. Extract into a table. One row per study: design, n, setting, effect, and the quote that supports the effect. AI extraction is fine; the quote makes it checkable.
  6. Verify the load-bearing studies. The 2 or 3 your conclusion rests on: 3-click each, and read them yourself, in full.
  7. Disclose. The AI tools used, at which stages, and how their output was verified. One paragraph in methods.
Where AI genuinely helpsWhere it must not decide
De-duplication, pre-screening, extraction into tables, summarizing a study's design, drafting the flow-diagram textFinal inclusion/exclusion calls, quality/risk-of-bias judgments, the conclusion, and any claim about a study you have not read
Reality check: a well-run AI-assisted screen of a few hundred abstracts is an afternoon instead of a fortnight. The verification of the studies that matter still takes the same evening it always did, and that is exactly as it should be.
