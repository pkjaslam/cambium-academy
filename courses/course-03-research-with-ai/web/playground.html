The Citation Hunt

What this trains: the eyeball test fails on purpose-built fakes, so the game gives you what reality gives you: limited time, limited checks, and a stack of plausible-looking references. The real ones here are real, famous papers you can open tonight. The fabricated ones were invented for this game, the same way an AI invents them: right shape, no substance. The Lateral Race

Five claims, ninety seconds. For each, pick the move a professional fact-checker would make. Wrong moves burn 8 seconds. Verify all five before the clock dies.

Remember SIFT: Stop · Investigate the source · Find better coverage · Trace to the original.
Best played after slides module 3.

Round 1/5
What this trains: the verification reflex. Reading down the page, re-asking the AI, and trusting the confident tone all feel productive, and all tell you nothing. The winning move is always lateral: leave the answer, open an independent tab, check the claim or the source there. With practice it really does take under a minute.

Receipts or Vibes

Same question, two AI answers. One earned your trust, one is performing confidence. Click the answer a careful researcher would build on. Watch for the trap rounds.

Receipts means sources you can follow AND that actually support the claim. A link that exists but says something else is still vibes.

What this trains: the receipts test. A grounded answer retrieves first, cites as it goes, and flags what it could not confirm. A vibes answer performs certainty. The tell is never the tone; it is whether you can follow every load-bearing claim to a source that really says it.
