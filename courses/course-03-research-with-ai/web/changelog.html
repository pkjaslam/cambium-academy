Course changelog

Every fact in this course is a dated snapshot. Snapshots age. So the course is reviewed on a schedule, and every change is written down here, in public, including the ones that make earlier versions look wrong.

13 JULY 2026 · v2.1

The Practical, the Pack, and the Data Clinic

  • Added The Practical: an optional honors exam that grades citation verdicts against the live Crossref registry, plus an agent-report audit. Passing adds a FIELD-VERIFIED mark to the certificate.
  • Added The Researcher's Pack: copy-ready prompts, per-venue disclosure statements, the checks, and a personal-stack template.
  • Added The Data Clinic: a real messy dataset with six planted traps and known answers, so the safe loop can be practised, not just described.
  • Wove the Riverbend case study through all ten modules (70 slides), and re-rendered the lecture film to 35 minutes so watchers and readers get the same course.
13 JULY 2026 · v2.0

From one trap to the whole toolkit

  • Rebuilt the course from 6 modules to 10: discovery agents, researcher prompting, verification, study design, AI data analysis (including qualitative work), multimodal, writing and peer review, reproducibility and reference integrity, responsible AI.
  • AI Lab grew from 3 games to 5 (added Pick Your Instrument and Prompt Surgery).
  • Aira's Classroom became student-aware: it reads your progress and drills what you keep missing.
  • Why: v1 taught the citation trap well but taught only that. A researcher needs the whole instrument rack, and where each instrument fails.
12 JULY 2026 · v1.0

Research with AI, first edition

  • Launched with the trust story: fabricated citations, lateral reading, the 3-click check, deep-research auditing.
  • That material survives as Module 4 and the Lab's first three games, because the numbers behind it only got worse.
Why a changelog on a free course? Because this course tells you to distrust confident, undated claims, and it would be absurd to be one. Every module's eye-opener is a measured fact with a date and a source on the resources page. When a rate changes, a tool dies, or a policy shifts, the fix lands here with the date attached. If you spot something stale before we do, say so on the community board: that is not a complaint, it is the course working.
