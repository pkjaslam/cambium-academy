What you will learn

Ten modules, one instrument each: a measured 2026 fact about where it shines, where it silently fails, and the one check that makes it safe. Keep your own project in mind; every module ends aimed at your work.

The 2026 research stack

An autonomous agent runs 1,500 papers in a day and gets one finding in five wrong. The instrument rack, and the twin rule: AI supplies speed, you supply trust.

Prompting for researchers

Context, exact deliverable, format, and demanded uncertainty. The four patterns that earn their keep, and why personas change tone, not truth.

Discover: the literature pipeline

Search-grounded answers, citation maps, deep research agents, standing alerts. Why academic specialists beat generalists, and the first-draft-with-leads rule.

Trust: the citation trap

1 in 277 papers now carries a fabricated reference. Lateral reading, SIFT, and the three clicks that catch essentially every fake.

18 min

Design before data

The cheapest AI hour in your project: power, sample size, test choice, confounds, pre-registration. Show the math or it did not happen.

14 min
6

Your AI data analyst

Plain English to working R, Python, SQL, MATLAB inside the safe loop, plus the qualitative lane: transcription and AI-proposed, human-adjudicated coding.

20 min

See and hear

Machine eyes for figures, micrographs, maps, and handwriting, with their measured limits. And your paper library talking back, with citations.

18 min
8

Write, translate, survive review

Revision, translation, grants, and pre-reviewing yourself. The 2026 policy table: Science, Nature, Elsevier, and NIH now genuinely differ.

18 min

Reproducible and intact

Documentation, pinned environments, and README files became a prompt. References come from the source of record, and every DOI must resolve.

Responsible AI + your stack

70% of journals have AI policies; 0.1% of papers disclose. The working code, the sensitive-data line, and your capstone: a one-page personal stack.
