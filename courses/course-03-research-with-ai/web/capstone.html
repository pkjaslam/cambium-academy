Capstone: one real question, fully verified

The quiz proves you understood the course. The capstone proves you can use it. Expect 45 to 90 minutes. Optional, but graduates who do it get the most out of the course, and the best write-ups get featured in the monthly AMA.

The brief

Pick one real question you actually need answered and research it with AI, start to finish, using the full course protocol. Good picks: a purchase you are about to make, a health or nutrition claim you keep hearing, a decision at work that needs evidence, a topic for a post or paper you owe someone, or a family debate that a real source would settle.
  1. Ask a search-grounded tool (or a deep research mode) and require receipts: sources you can click.
  2. Identify the 2 or 3 load-bearing claims your answer rests on.
  3. Verify each one laterally: a new tab, an independent source, SIFT.
  4. Run the 3-click check on every citation that matters: exists, matches, says it. Note anything that fails.
  5. Write it up (300 to 500 words): the question, which tool you used, your verification trail, anything the AI got wrong or invented and how you caught it, and your final, sourced answer.
