1 · The prompts that earn their keep

Paste these verbatim. They work in any chatbot.

The standing uncertainty sentence (use on every factual ask)

Flag what you are not sure of, state the assumptions you just made, and tell me what I should verify before relying on this.

Reviewer 2, on demand (before you submit)

Attack this manuscript as a skeptical reviewer in my field: clarity, methods gaps, statistical red flags, overclaimed conclusions, and missing limitations. Be specific and merciless. For each problem, say what evidence or revision would fix it.

The methods consult (before data exists)

Here is my study: [design, groups, n, outcome, constraints]. Recommend the appropriate analysis, state every assumption it depends on, show the sample-size math in full, list the three most likely fatal design flaws, and say what a hostile reviewer would propose instead. Flag anything I must verify with a statistician.

The safe analysis loop (coding assistants)

Data: [what each column means, units, what one row represents]. Goal: [the analysis and the plot I need]. Constraints: [language, packages, must-nots]. Rules: comment every block; explain each line before I run it; start with a tiny test on 10 rows I can hand-check; then scale. If a choice is statistically debatable, say so and give the alternative.

The agent-report audit (after any deep research run)

List the 2 or 3 sources this report's main conclusion actually rests on, quote the exact sentence each one supports, and give the DOI or direct link for each. Then tell me, for each: does it exist, does it match the description, and does it say what the report claims?

2 · Disclosure statements, per venue

Adapt the bracketed parts. Always check your venue's live policy page at submission; these follow the July 2026 rules taught in Module 8.

Science-style (all AI use disclosed, in acknowledgments)

The authors used [tool and version] to [specific tasks: e.g. draft revision, language editing, code generation for Figure 2]. All AI-assisted output was reviewed and verified by the authors, who take full responsibility for the content of this manuscript.

Nature-portfolio-style (methods-level, substantive use; copy-editing exempt)

[Tool and version] was used during [analysis / code development / literature screening], as described in Methods. No AI-generated images were used. The authors verified all outputs and are accountable for the accuracy and integrity of the work.

Elsevier-style (dedicated declaration)

Declaration of generative AI and AI-assisted technologies in the writing process: During the preparation of this work the author(s) used [tool] in order to [reason]. After using this tool, the author(s) reviewed and edited the content as needed and take full responsibility for the content of the publication.

Grants / NIH-aware (proposals)

AI-assisted tools were used only for [language editing / formatting / literature triage]. The scientific premise, aims, design, and interpretation are the investigators' own. No confidential or unpublished third-party material was submitted to any AI system. [Note: NIH bars reviewers from entering applications into AI tools, and proposals substantially written by AI may be rejected.]

3 · The checks, on one page

The 3-click citation check

ClickQuestionHowIf it fails
1Does it exist?Paste the DOI at doi.org, or the title into Google Scholar / CrossrefFabricated. Delete it.
2Is it what it claims?Check authors, venue, year against the recordMisattributed. Fix or delete.
3Does it say it?Open the abstract; find the sentence that supports the claimOverstated. Rewrite the claim.

Data sensitivity: what may touch a cloud chatbot?

Is it published or fully public? Yes → cloud tools are fine.
Is it pre-publication (yours)? Use a tool with training opt-out; never paste co-authors' or reviewers' confidential material.
Is it someone else's unpublished work (a manuscript or grant you are reviewing)? Never. NIH bars this outright.
Human subjects, identifiable, or IRB-governed? Never in a consumer chatbot. Use a local model, an institution-hosted deployment, or fully de-identified/synthetic stand-in data, and follow your IRB.
Rule of thumb: if it could not go in an email to a stranger, it does not go in a cloud chatbot.

Verification by stakes

StakesWhat you do
Low (trivia, brainstorming)Trust freely. Enjoy the speed.
Medium (an email fact, a purchase)Sixty seconds: verify the one load-bearing claim laterally.
High (grades, money, health, law, anything published)Full protocol: grounded tools, lateral reading, every citation 3-clicked, disclosure written.
