## Learning outcomes

By the end, a learner can turn a vague request into a structured ask (task, context, format) that gets a usable answer on the first try, choose between an instant mode and a thinking mode and know when the extra wait pays off, control tone and output shape by showing a sample instead of describing it, use the three steering habits (phrase positively, give the model permission to say "I'm not sure," and treat the first draft as a draft), set up a personal workspace (a Project, Gem, or Custom GPT) with their own material and inspect or delete what the AI remembers, and verify an AI answer in under a minute with lateral reading while spotting leading questions and hidden-instruction tricks.

## Modules

1. **Why answers vary so much.** The era of magic words is over; structure is what still moves the needle. Instant versus thinking modes and when each pays off. (10 min)
2. **The anatomy of a great ask.** Give it a job not a topic, tell it what it cannot know, and say what the answer should look like: task, context, format. Which famous tips to drop. (14 min)
3. **Show, don't describe.** Why everything sounds like AI, pasting the tone you want, and ordering the exact shape of the output. (10 min)
4. **Steering, and second drafts.** The pink elephant problem, giving the model an out, when "think step by step" helps, and never shipping the first draft. (12 min)
5. **Your material, and its memory.** Open book not training, the three rooms (chat, project, custom assistant), where to place documents so they are read, and what the model remembers about you. (12 min)
6. **When to trust the answer.** Confidence is not accuracy, the sixty-second lateral-reading check, not leading the witness, spotting hidden instructions, and disclosing AI help. (14 min)

Then the 20-question quiz and the certificate.

## Fact discipline

Prompting advice ages fast, so this course was researched the week it was written (facts checked 2026-07-09, sources cited inline in `course-content.md`). It teaches the durable ideas (tiers, modes, structure, showing an example, verification habits) and treats fast-moving model names as footnotes to re-check before recording. Four items are flagged for a human pass before recording; see the Production Notes at the end of `course-content.md`. This course should be refreshed when the landscape shifts.
