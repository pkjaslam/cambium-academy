What you will learn

Six short modules. One idea per slide, plain English throughout. By the end you can write an ask that works on the first try, and know when to trust the answer.

1

Why answers vary so much

The era of magic words is over. Meet your brilliant intern, and the one switch, instant versus thinking mode, that most people never touch.

10 min
2

The anatomy of a great ask

Task, context, format: the three parts of a request that works. Plus the famous "act as an expert" tip you should drop for facts.

14 min
3

Show, don't describe

The fastest way to control tone and shape is to show one example. Why everything sounds like AI, and the one-line fix.

10 min
4

Steering, and second drafts

The pink elephant problem, giving the model an out, when "think step by step" still helps, and never shipping the first draft.

12 min
5

Your material, and its memory

Hand over the folder: Projects, Gems, and Custom GPTs. Where to place key documents, and how to read or delete what the AI remembers about you.

12 min
6

When to trust the answer

Confidence is not accuracy. Verify in sixty seconds with lateral reading, keep your conclusion out of the question, and catch a smuggled instruction.

14 min
